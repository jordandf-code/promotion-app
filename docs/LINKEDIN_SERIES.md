# LinkedIn / Blog Post Series — Plan

## Goal

Build external eminence through an 8-post series over 4 weeks (~2 posts/week). Tell the story of building a career command center as a non-engineer using AI, and explore what it means for the future of software.

## Principles

- **Written by Jordan, not AI.** Claude helps with structure, narrative planning, and rewrites — but the voice is human. AI-generated LinkedIn posts read like AI-generated LinkedIn posts. Everyone can tell.
- **Show real artifacts.** Code snippets, curated Claude Code conversations, screenshots. Make it tangible and credible.
- **Be honest about what works and what doesn't.** Credibility comes from the failures post as much as the wins.
- **Vulnerability is a strength.** "I'm going for Partner" is bold. It's also memorable and relatable.

## The narrative arc

Start broad (the build), get personal (the why), go deep (the how), then zoom out (the implications).

## Series outline

### Week 1: The hook

**Post 1 — "I built a production app in 3 days and I'm not a developer."**
- Lead with the scope: multi-user auth, AI integration, PWA, email notifications, 13 tabs of functionality
- The punchline: I'm a management consultant. I don't have a CS degree.
- Broad appeal, shareable. This is the attention-grabber.
- Include: screenshot of the app, maybe the feature list

**Post 2 — "Why I built it: I'm going for Partner and I had no system."**
- The vulnerable one. "I'm going for a major promotion and I realized I had no structured way to track my case."
- Started as a promotion tracker, quickly became something bigger
- Anchor: "I started by looking at my promotion journey and it quickly evolved into something bigger but still very personal"
- This hooks the professional services crowd specifically

### Week 2: The how

**Post 3 — "What working with AI-assisted development actually looks like."**
- Show real Claude Code back-and-forth conversations
- Curated examples: describing a feature in plain English → Claude scaffolding it → course corrections
- Talk about the CLAUDE.md file — how you "manage" the AI with project instructions
- Core coding philosophies and what you do that works
- Make it practical and real, not theoretical

**Post 4 — "What doesn't work."**
- The honest post. PowerPoint generation is rough. Sometimes the AI goes in circles.
- Show a real failure and how you handled it
- When to push back vs. when to change approach
- What you've learned about directing AI when you don't fully understand the code yourself
- This builds massive credibility

### Week 3: The bigger picture

**Post 5 — "From promotion tracker to career command center."**
- The vision post. How the tool expanded beyond its original scope
- The concept of campaigns, post-promotion evolution, sharing with peers at other firms
- Product thinking without engineering background — domain expertise driving design

**Post 6 — "The SaaS reckoning."**
- The provocative take. "If I can build this in a weekend, what happens to the $50/month tools that do less?"
- What this means for niche SaaS, small business tech stacks, the software industry
- The long tail of CRUD + business logic apps is vulnerable
- This is the thought leadership cornerstone — the post people share and debate

### Week 4: The implications

**Post 7 — "The new skill stack."**
- What it actually takes to build with AI: product thinking, clear communication, domain expertise, taste
- It's not about coding — it's about knowing what to build
- The value hierarchy has flipped: domain expertise > engineering skill for a huge category of software
- "The new moat is not code. It's knowing your problem space deeply."

**Post 8 — "What would you build?"**
- The closer. Bring it back to the reader.
- "You have domain expertise that no engineer has. What's the tool you wish existed?"
- Invite engagement, close the series on an empowering note
- Call to action is a question, not a pitch

## Content to weave in across posts

- Code snippets (real, not polished)
- Claude Code conversation screenshots showing the back-and-forth
- The CLAUDE.md management approach
- App screenshots showing actual functionality
- Specific numbers: 23 phases, 3 days, lines of code, etc.
- Failures alongside successes
